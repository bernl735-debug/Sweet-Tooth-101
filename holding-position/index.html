My Favorite Ecuadorian Desserts

Here are some of my favorite Ecuadorian desserts! I decided to share a little about my culture and the delicious treats that are popular where I grew up.

1. Tres Leches is a sponge cake soaked in three kinds of milk: evaporated milk, condensed milk, and heavy cream. It's incredibly moist and sweet, often topped with whipped cream and fresh fruit.

2. Espumilla is a light and airy dessert made from whipped egg whites, sugar, and fruit pulp, typically guava or passion fruit. It's similar to meringue but has a fruity twist.

3. Guaguas de Pan are sweet bread rolls shaped like little babies, traditionally made for the Day of the Dead celebrations. They are often decorated with colorful icing and sugar.

4. Helado de Paila is a traditional Ecuadorian ice cream made by churning fruit juice and sugar in a large, shallow copper pan over ice. It's refreshing and comes in various fruit flavors.

5. Quimbolito is a steamed corn cake wrapped in banana leaves, often enjoyed as a snack or dessert. It's sweet and has a unique, moist texture.
